About Parrexion Games

Parrexion Games consist of Alexander Frisk, a solo programmer, who is developing interesting twists on familiar game ideas. The games are in 2D or 3D with a 2D-locked perspective on the camera.

Alexander Frisk, aka Parrexion
Dragon, Indie developer, and Programmer.
Also speaks Japanese and plays the piano.

Gamedev journey

I've making games my whole life all the way back in elementary when I was making up different games of make believe I would play together with my two brothers and friends. Once I sat down in Gamemaker 6 in 6th grade I was hooked by programming. I didn't really make any real video games until high school when I made a complete digital board game as my high school project.
But it wasn't until the last year of university that I actually realised that I could be working on making video games full time that I started taking it seriously. I found a job at Zordix making racing games for about 4 years using Unity. But now I'm at the point that I want to try to go full indie instead.

Work experience

4 years of working on games in Unity at Zordix and Zordix Racing which also included some javascript for handling the servers. Leart a lot of Java in university but haven't worked on a commersial project using it so far.

Favourite games

For me, what's important in games is the experience itself. That is both the story, the challenge and, last but not least, the music.
The story is important because video games tells stories in a completely different way from all other mediums.
The challenge is important because I'm a problem solver and solving difficult and interesting tasks gives me a kick.
The music is also important because it always directly connects to my emotions.

My top 3 consists of "The World Ends With You", "Ace Attorney: Trials and Tribulations", and "Zero Escape: Virtue's Last Reward".
